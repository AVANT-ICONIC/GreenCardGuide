# Product Requirements Document

## Product name
Consular Prep

## One-line description
A bilingual interview-prep system that helps immigrant visa applicants and sponsors know exactly what documents to bring, what to expect, and what common mistakes can delay them.

## Initial scope
- **Post:** Ciudad Juárez
- **Case family:** Family-based immigrant visa interviews
- **Audience:** Applicants, petitioners/sponsors, family members helping organize documents
- **Languages:** English and Spanish

## Problem statement
Applicants regularly arrive underprepared because guidance is fragmented, stressful to interpret, and not presented in a practical case-aware format. Many users do not know:

- what is required today versus helpful backup
- what needs to be printed
- what must be original versus copied
- what changes when a joint sponsor, derivative child, or prior marriage is involved
- what behavior or entry rules can create problems on interview day

## Product thesis
Do not build a generic immigration guide. Build a **practical, bilingual, case-aware prep workflow**.

## User segments

### 1. Principal applicant
Needs a calm, clear, phone-friendly prep guide.

### 2. Sponsor / petitioner
Needs to gather financial and support documents correctly.

### 3. Family helper
Needs to cross-check readiness for another person.

## Jobs to be done
- Tell me exactly what to bring.
- Tell me what to print before I travel.
- Tell me what I need for medical, ASC, and interview.
- Tell me what is original versus copy.
- Warn me about common mistakes.
- Help me prepare even if I am stressed and reading on my phone.

## Core value proposition
"Know exactly what to print, pack, and bring."

## Product principles
1. Official-source anchored
2. Bilingual by design
3. Stress-proof and mobile-first
4. Case-aware
5. Action-first, not essay-first
6. Conservative wording
7. Visible updates and review dates

## MVP goals
The MVP should deliver four things exceptionally well:

1. Ciudad Juárez prep hub
2. Personalized checklist builder
3. Interview-week timeline / prep sequence
4. Common turn-away mistakes page

## MVP non-goals
- broad nationwide coverage
- all immigrant/nonimmigrant visa types
- legal strategy advice
- community/forum content as primary truth
- unconstrained AI assistant

## Key features

### Personalized checklist builder
Inputs:
- case family
- post
- principal vs derivative
- children immigrating
- prior marriages
- joint sponsor
- arrests/court records if applicable
- passport readiness
- translations concern

Outputs:
- required documents
- conditional documents
- backup documents
- print-before-travel list
- interview-week prep list
- risk flags

### Post-specific hub
A canonical page for Ciudad Juárez with:
- what to bring
- step sequence
- medical and ASC prep
- what not to bring
- common mistakes
- FAQ

### Print mode
Users can generate:
- personalized checklist
- night-before checklist
- folder packing sheet

### Trust layer
Each page should show:
- last reviewed date
- source references
- confidence category
- informational / not legal advice notice

## Success metrics
Primary:
- checklist completion rate
- print/save rate
- self-reported preparedness
- return visits during interview week

Secondary:
- most common confusion points
- most common missing-document flags
- language split
- referral rate

## Future roadmap directions
- more case branches
- more posts
- structured CMS/admin
- source change monitoring
- constrained AI assistant over approved content
